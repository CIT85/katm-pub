🥀What to do when you're alone🥀

When the world goes quiet and you're the only one left to hear it, the silence can be louder than any scream. Being alone in the apocalypse isn’t just about survival—it’s about learning how to stay human when everything familiar is gone. Whether you’ve lost your group, chosen solitude, or never had anyone to begin with, this moment is yours. This is where your story continues—not just to endure, but to find strength, comfort, and even beauty in the stillness.



🌷How to Feel Less Lonely (Even When You're All Alone)🌷

Adopt a Companion
A dog, cat, or even a well-behaved chicken can provide comfort, routine, and cuddles.
Keep a Journal
Write your thoughts, doodle your day, or invent stories. It’s like talking to a future version of you.
Read a Book
Escape into another world, learn something new, or reread your favorites like old friends.
Talk Out Loud
Narrate your actions, sing, or have pretend conversations. It helps keep your voice strong and your mind sharper.
Make a "Friend"
Seriously—build a plush, draw a face on a pillow, or decorate a mannequin. Name them. Talk to them
Create a Routine
Structure gives you a sense of purpose and control, even if the world outside is chaos.
Grow Something
A small plant, garden, or herb pot can offer beauty and responsibility. Bonus: food.
Stay Active
Stretch, dance, run drills—movement fights off gloom and gets those feel-good chemicals going.
Collect Keepsakes
Save little treasures or mementos from your travels. They’re reminders you’ve been places.
Leave Notes (Even for Yourself)
Hide kind or funny messages in places you’ll later find. It’s like time-traveling friendship.
Practice a Skill
Drawing, braiding, knife throwing—anything that gives you a sense of progress.
Talk to the Sky
Whether it’s to loved ones lost, the universe, or nothing at all… saying things out loud matters.
